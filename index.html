Gustavo Villarruel

I am a Masters Student with a Fulbright scholarship at the State University of New York at Binghamton in the Department of Geological Sciences, where I am advised by Dr. Jeffrey T. Pietras. My masters thesis focuses on the sedimentology and XRF-based chemostratigraphy of the Trenton Group carbonates in the Taconic Foreland Basin in New York.

I am a geoscientist with a great interest in both natural and anthropogenic hazards. For my undergraduate dissertation I studied the effects of wildfires on soils from the Calamuchita Valley, Argentina. After graduating with a BS in Geology (honors) from the National University of Cordoba, I was a Junior Geologist at Represas Patagonia. I enjoyed carrying out field measurements to monitor the stability of the slopes of the Condor Cliff Dam and prevent potential landslides.

Outside of the office, you'll find me hiking, practicing swimming, or cooking Argentinian dishes and pastries.
